The Layer Method™

The Layer Method™

Calibrae’s diagnosis-first framework for understanding what is actually driving a concern, then treating the right layer in the right order.

Request Consultation Explore Skin

Treat the driver, not just the complaint.

Most patients talk about what they see, heaviness, lines, dullness, sagging, skin changes, or that something just feels off. The Layer Method™ looks deeper to understand what is actually causing that concern before deciding what treatment makes sense.

A more intelligent way to plan care

Sometimes the issue is movement. Sometimes it is support loss. Sometimes it is skin quality. Sometimes it is tissue change, proportion, or a combination. The Layer Method™ helps organize that thinking so care is guided with more precision and less guesswork.

How it shows up across the practice

Skin: barrier health, resilience, pigment, texture, and long-term quality. Injectables: movement, support, balance, and sequencing. Structural planning: assessing the bigger picture. Regenerative care: tissue support beyond simple correction. Refinement: smaller finishing adjustments after the main driver is addressed.

Where structure comes in

Calibrae also uses a structural assessment lens to help identify when support loss, facial framework changes, or deeper architecture are contributing to what a patient sees. That helps make sure the visible complaint is not being treated in the wrong category.

Diagnosis guides the plan. Home care supports the result.

The Layer Method™ helps determine what is driving the concern. The Calibrae regimen helps support the result at home through barrier support, correction, resilience, recovery, and maintenance.

Injectables

Injectables, planned with more intention.

At Calibrae, injectable treatment is guided by anatomy, movement, structure, proportion, tissue quality, and a clearer understanding of what is actually driving the concern.

Request Consultation

Not every concern needs the same kind of treatment.

Sometimes the issue is movement. Sometimes it is support loss. Sometimes it is proportion. Sometimes it is tissue quality. Sometimes it is skin quality. Sometimes it is a combination. That is part of The Layer Method™ in practice.

When movement is the driver

Neurotoxin helps soften overactive pull patterns, reduce repetitive movement that deepens certain lines over time, and create a more balanced, rested appearance without disconnecting the face from expression.

When structure needs support

This is where facial balancing, chin and jawline balancing, and support-focused placement matter. The goal is not to add volume for the sake of volume. It is to restore or support architecture with purpose.

When the tissue itself needs support

Some patients benefit from a regenerative layer, where PRF and EZ Gel may play a role in supporting tissue quality, healing environment, and longer-term rejuvenation.

Refinement comes after the foundation

The most polished outcomes usually do not come from chasing one isolated detail first. They come from understanding what needs to be addressed before refinement, then making smaller adjustments in a way that still looks natural and balanced.
