Overview

The Layer Method™

Diagnosis first. Treatment second.

The Layer Method™ is Calibrae’s diagnosis-first framework for understanding what is truly driving a concern before choosing treatment. Instead of reacting to the surface complaint alone, treatment is guided by structure, movement, skin quality, and sequencing.

01

Diagnosis before product

Treatment starts with understanding the cause, not reaching for a product first.

02

Structure before refinement

A stronger foundation creates more balanced, natural-looking results.

03

Treatment by cause, not impulse

What bothers you most is not always what should be treated first.

04

The right treatment, in the right layer, in the right order

Better outcomes come from treating the correct driver in the correct sequence.

The goal is not to do more. The goal is to do what actually belongs.

Philosophy

Your skin is an organ. Treat it like one.

Healthy skin is not built through random products, rushed routines, or constant overcorrection. Calibrae approaches skin with the same philosophy used throughout the practice: understand what is happening first, then choose the right level of support with more clarity and more intention.

Better skin starts with better structure, better support, and better sequencing.

Framework

Protect. Correct. Build.

A more intentional way to organize skin care.

Calibrae’s skin system is built around three core functions: protect what the skin needs, correct what is actively driving concern, and build healthier skin quality over time.

Protect

Barrier support, cleansing, hydration, lipid balance, and daily SPF help create the conditions skin needs to function well.

Correct

Pigment, texture, acne, uneven tone, and other visible concerns are approached more thoughtfully when the skin is ready for correction.

Build

Long-term skin quality depends on consistency, collagen support, resilience, and a plan that strengthens the skin over time rather than constantly stressing it.

The goal is not to overload the skin. The goal is to support it intelligently.

Approach

Diagnosis before product.

Calibrae does not begin with a syringe. Treatment starts with assessment. The goal is to understand what is truly driving a concern before deciding whether neurotoxin, filler, regenerative support, or staging makes the most sense.

The best result starts with the right diagnosis.

Framework

Movement. Support. Sequence.

A more thoughtful way to plan injectable care.

Visible concerns are not always caused by the thing patients notice first. Some concerns are driven by expression patterns. Some reflect volume loss or structural decline. Others are shaped by skin quality or changes that need to be staged over time.

Movement

Neurotoxin is used to address expression patterns, muscular pull, and dynamic contributors when movement is the true driver.

Support

Filler and supportive treatments are used to restore contour, proportion, and structure when support loss is the bigger issue.

Sequence

Not every concern should be treated at once. Many of the best outcomes come from choosing the right order rather than trying to do everything in one visit.

The goal is not more product. The goal is better placement and better planning.

Hair

Hair, approached with more intention.

A medically informed approach to thinning, shedding, density changes, and long-term hair support.

At Calibrae, hair concerns are approached the same way as every other treatment plan — with assessment first. Rather than treating hair loss as a quick-fix cosmetic issue, Calibrae looks at pattern, timing, scalp environment, and realistic treatment pathways to build a more thoughtful plan.

Diagnosis-First Hair Support

Hair plans are often built in layers, not chosen as a one-step fix.

Assessment, foundational support, and staged treatment planning matter more than rushed intervention.

Assessment first

Hair concerns are not all the same. Thinning, shedding, reduced density, and changes around the hairline can have different drivers, timelines, and treatment needs. The first step is understanding what is actually happening before deciding how to treat it.

Build the foundation

Healthy hair begins with a healthier environment. Calibrae’s approach considers scalp condition, follicular support, consistency, and long-term maintenance rather than relying on one isolated intervention.

Treatment in steps

Hair concerns are rarely improved through one isolated intervention alone. Most patients benefit from a staged approach that may begin with assessment and foundational support, then build into more targeted treatment over time. Calibrae approaches hair the same way it approaches every other concern: understand the driver first, then build the right plan in the right order.

Step 1 — Assess

Pattern, timing, shedding behavior, scalp condition, and treatment history

Step 2 — Build

Scalp support, follicular support, consistency, and foundational care

Step 3 — Support Over Time

Regenerative options, maintenance planning, and long-term follow-through when appropriate

A personalized plan

Some patients need guidance on where to begin. Others may need a more layered strategy over time. Calibrae builds hair plans with the same philosophy used throughout the practice: understand the driver first, then choose the right level of support.

Better hair support comes from a more thoughtful plan — not a rushed fix.

Founder & Provider

Danielle Wiley, PA-C

Founder, Calibrae Institute of Aesthetic Medicine

Danielle Wiley, PA-C is a board-certified physician assistant specializing in advanced aesthetic and regenerative treatments. Known for her structured, anatomy-driven approach, she focuses on identifying the true cause of a concern rather than treating it superficially. As the founder of Calibrae Institute of Aesthetic Medicine and creator of The Layer Method™, Danielle is recognized for delivering natural, balanced results through thoughtful, precision-based care.

Danielle Wiley, PA-C is a board-certified physician assistant specializing in advanced aesthetic and regenerative treatments. Her approach is rooted in clinical precision, structural understanding, and a philosophy that prioritizes treating the underlying cause—not just the visible concern.

Her path into medicine began with a personal commitment to health and wellness, where she developed a deep appreciation for the connection between discipline, physical change, and long-term outcomes. That foundation continued during her time as a collegiate athlete, playing rugby at Penn State University while studying kinesiology and contributing to a national championship team—an experience that continues to influence her performance-driven, anatomy-focused mindset.

Danielle's early clinical experience was shaped within regenerative medicine at National Spine & Pain Centers in Tysons Corner, Virginia, where she worked under the mentorship of Dr. Mayo Friedlis. Over several years, she developed a strong foundation in injection-based therapies, sterile technique, and protocol-driven care, while gaining an appreciation for thoughtful, patient-centered treatment and ethical clinical decision-making.

She went on to earn her Master of Physician Assistant Studies from Alderson Broaddus University, where she trained in a hands-on clinical environment and completed advanced cadaver-based anatomical coursework. This experience deepened her understanding of facial structure, anatomical variation, and precision-based injection techniques.

Danielle's background also includes several years in family medicine with a focus on medical weight loss and GLP-1 therapy. This experience provided her with a unique understanding of the structural and facial changes associated with weight loss, further informing her approach to comprehensive, long-term aesthetic planning.

Prior to founding Calibrae, Danielle played a central role in developing and expanding an established aesthetic practice, where she helped build the injectable department and introduce a range of advanced treatment services. This experience further refined her clinical systems, consultation approach, and commitment to intentional, outcomes-driven care.

Her work today is guided by The Layer Method™, a diagnosis-first framework designed to identify the true anatomical drivers of a concern and treat them in the correct layer and sequence. She is known for a conservative, structure-first approach that prioritizes balance, longevity, and natural results.

Through Calibrae, Danielle is building a practice centered on thoughtful medicine, elevated aesthetics, and a higher standard of care.
